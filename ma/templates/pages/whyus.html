Why Children





Poverty is one of the reasons why the majority of the children worldwide are deprived of many of their rights. Uganda has one of the fastest growing populations globally 3.4% annually and has the second most youthful population in the world with a median age of just 15.7 years old. The National Financial Inclusion Strategy (NFIS) 2017-2022 which is Uganda’s holistic strategy for promoting financial inclusion places emphasis on these pillars: Reduce financial exclusion and barriers to access financial services, Build the digital infrastructure, Deepen and broaden formal savings, investment and insurance usage.


Kids as young as 9 regularly accumulate money but do not know how to actively manage it, and need a safe place to save it. Building online digital and business infrastructure can improve proximity and bring the would be harder-to-reach clients into the financial system at potentially lower costs.

We believe that the development of financial management skills will lead to an increased tendency among youths to, create businesses of their own, and become good decision makers able to meet future goals – all of which have broader implications for reducing poverty.
